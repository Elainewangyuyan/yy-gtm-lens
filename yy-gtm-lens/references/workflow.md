## 1. Operating modes

### Full pipeline

Use when the user asks for a finished report or names only a company:

`Researcher → Research QA → user-approved text → Designer → Visual QA → publish`

### Text-first

Use when the user wants to inspect the research before design:

1. Research and draft.
2. Independent QA.
3. Revise.
4. Deliver public and private text.
5. Wait for approval.
6. Render web and PDFs.

### Update

Use for changing an existing company report:

1. Recheck time-sensitive facts.
2. Update JSON.
3. Repeat QA on changed and dependent sections.
4. Regenerate every artifact.

## 2. Researcher

The researcher:

- Establishes the exact cutoff date.
- Builds a source map before writing.
- Separates facts, disclosures, evaluations, inferences and unknowns.
- Writes concise Chinese rather than formal corporate prose.
- Connects product and technical facts to business and GTM implications.
- Creates prioritized, measurable and low-cost growth recommendations.
- Keeps personal career analysis in section 09 only.

Required public coverage:

- Company history and strategic change.
- Competitive landscape.
- Product and monetization.
- Users, customers, revenue and growth signals.
- Funding, valuation, investors/shareholders and capital efficiency.
- Model, data, evaluation and open/closed strategy.
- Founders, organization, hiring, careers link and GTM reporting line.
- GTM bottlenecks and 0–90 day actions.
- Catalysts, risks and watch metrics.

## 3. Research QA

Use a fresh reviewer when possible. Give it the draft and source list, not the intended answer.

The reviewer:

- Challenges every high-risk number.
- Checks whether historical metrics are presented as current.
- Verifies event date, publication date and access date.
- Checks proposed versus completed financing.
- Tests whether advice follows from evidence.
- Flags missing information that matters to founders or a prospective GTM leader.
- Returns P0/P1/P2 findings plus a revised recommendation.

The researcher must either fix every P0/P1 issue or explicitly mark the information unknown.

## 4. Designer

The designer uses the reviewed JSON only.

- Preserve wording and section order.
- Place judgment before detail.
- Use one main visual per page.
- Split only at a heading, paragraph or complete list boundary.
- Keep owner views editable and comments web-only.
- Keep section 09 physically separate.
- Use the visual type mapped to each research section unless the evidence demands another form.

## 5. Visual QA

Visual QA is independent from the designer.

- Inspect the actual user path, not a substitute artifact.
- Compare file generation times with the latest code/style edit.
- Review a full contact sheet and enlarged risk pages.
- Measure title, tags, signals, body and visual boundaries.
- Test desktop, mobile, browser print, programmatic PDF and private PDF.
- Reject any overlap, clipping, unreadable label, false alignment or private leak.

## 6. Approval and handoff

Deliver:

- Exact research cutoff date.
- Reviewed public and private text.
- QA status and unresolved unknowns.
- Website entry point.
- Public and private PDF links.
- Contact sheet or QA evidence.
- Safe public bundle path.

Do not claim release readiness unless research QA and visual QA both pass.
